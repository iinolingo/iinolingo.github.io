Color Converter Used: I used the W3 Schools color converter!

Hexadecimal codes are values that tell how much the color displayed is gonna show, the values range from 0 to 255. There are 16 million different colors! But RGB uses 8 bits for the red, green, and blue color combinations. It inputs as rgbrgb(230, 255, 204) rather than #e6ffcc. HSL stands for Hue, Saturation, and Lightness. This is a much more intuitive way though. Hue is determined by the degrees around the circle, saturation is made by the percentage between 0 and 100%. The closer to 100 the less gray. Lightness is pretty self explanatory! It looks like hsl(89, 100%, 90%).

Preferred color code format: I prefer to use hexadecimal codes for the colors because I feel like it is a lot easier to type, plus it is easier for me to understand!

HWB summary: Hue, Whiteness, and Blackness! Its like HSL and the hue can be anywhere within 0 and 360. The other 2 percentages are how much white or black is mixed in.

CMYK summary: Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, and Black are what make up CMYK. The amount of those in percentage is what will determine the color.
